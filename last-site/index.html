amy rae fox

Cognitive/Information/Learning Sciences · Research/Design

I study Human-Information Interaction.
At the intersection of cognitive, information, and learning sciences, I explore how humans create and appropriate tools for thinking. These cognitive technologies range from stunning interactive visualizations of data, simple charts and graphs, diagrams, systems of notation, to even the text on this screen.

I leverage what we know about human cognition to inform the design of tools for thinking, and study their everyday use to learn about human cognition.

I am presently working as a Postdoctoral Fellow in the MIT Visualization Group, jointly supported by a CSAIL METEOR Fellowship, and MIT School of Engineering Postdoctoral Fellowship for Engineering Excellence (PFPEE).
